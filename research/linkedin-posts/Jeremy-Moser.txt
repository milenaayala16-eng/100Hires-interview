I've never been a fan of turning off systems that are producing results for you right now.

People look at their data, see good SEO leads coming in, then spot a shiny object in the corner. AI search. Reddit. Whatever's new. And they want to divert the budget into it.

But if you have to turn off something that's working to invest in something that's a hit or miss, you're just hindering your long-term results.

Here's the split I recommend for most companies right now.

Invest 70% of your budget in traditional SEO. The rest into GEO-specific tactics and PR, which is producing good results right now.

And if you're not already established in SEO? You need even more than 70% there.

A lot of brands come in with zero SEO success and want to invest everything in AI search. That's a mistake. You need the foundation before GEO pays off long term.

New company? Go heavy on SEO. Then run your short-term AI search tactics off-site, where you can move quickly without risking what you’ve built.

Most people comparing AI search leads to SEO leads are measuring the wrong things.

Here's the problem.

The valuable leads from AI search almost always come from bottom of the funnel. Someone asks for a solution, AI spits out a few options, and that's where the good quality leads come from.

But people compare that narrow slice to the entire swath of SEO. Top of funnel, mid funnel, all of it.

The comparison is broken and flawed from the start.

And then folks are making massive budget decisions based on that data.

Peel back the layers. Look at your BOFU from SEO and just the BOFU from AI search. Compare those. What we've seen for ourselves and across clients is that the lead quality is actually comparable.

There's a lot of misinformation in the AI space right now. Not because the leads aren't good. We're just comparing the wrong things to each other.

Stop making budget calls on a whim based on murky data.

POST 3
================================================================================

Stop exploiting GEO tactics for short term gains. They always backfire.

Our own AI visibility increased the most by doing one thing: acquiring mentions and links on publications talking about our niche. Being in more places that AI pulls from.

That means your site, third party sites, reviews, directories, social, round ups, comparisons.

Kevin Indig said it best: “SEO is the skeleton of the body that is AEO.”

Creating consensus to show up in AI comes from great off-page SEO and brand building.

LLMs sometimes require more specific approaches on top of solid SEO fundamentals.

Certain links and mentions increase LLM citations more than others. Meaning you CAN tailor a strategy towards GEO within the SEO discipline itself.

Rank well organically, you show up more in AI search. Optimize well for AI search, it lifts your SEO too.
