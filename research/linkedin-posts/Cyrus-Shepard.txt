Most ranking declines in 2026 aren't about technical SEO, but the types of content Google now rewards

So I built a "Competitive SEO Pattern Report" - using Agent A 🤖

https://lnkd.in/gnyB-WpY

Almost weekly, website owners reach out with the same problem - a decline in Google traffic to scary levels 📉 🥲

The problem is *almost never* technical SEO - it's that Google simply rewards a different type of content now than "Commodity content + links"

If you face a similar ranking decline, there's a good chance Google thinks your content is "commodity" or "generic."

To figure out what to do instead, here is a process I use:

1) Identify your winning and losing pages
2) Find competitors with the largest keyword overlap
3) Analyze both the URLs gaining and losing visibility across those sites
4) Classify pages by type: product, blog, comparison, research, news, how-to, definition, template, tool, etc
5) Finally, score the content against patterns we see working in Google SERPs - commodity vs. non-commodity, brand, experience, first-party info, freshness, task completion, format, etc

Honestly, it's a lot of work, but showing this to clients is typically eye-opening

I've done this process manually many times. So when our friends at Ahrefs asked if I wanted to partner to build something with Agent A - I knew this is what I wanted to build

The app uses live Ahrefs data to gather all metrics, find competitors, and evaluate content against our predefined criteria.

For now, the app lives in my own Agent A account, but you could easily build something similar

Looking forward to what I can build next. Thanks for partnering!

Google says it "ignores" llms txt, so I was curious whether this is a 100% hard rule. Does it really ignore them for SEO purposes?

Upon testing, Google obviously:

- knows about llms txt
- reads and displays info from them
- can cite llms txt files in responses

In this particular example, the llms txt file is the ONLY URL cited for this specific topic

So no, it appears Google does not completely "ignore" llms txt

At least, not entirely.

Here's a thought experiment: What would Google do if you made your llms txt file the homepage of your website? 🤔 🔬
